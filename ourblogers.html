The things we wish were true

"This book isn't going to win any great literature awards, but it is a pleasant and quick read - perfect for a day by the pool or at the beach. It's the story of life and drama in the 'burbs, as told by several of the neighbors. The author's writing style is straightforward and easy to read, and the story does have a couple twists. The characters are well written, I felt like I knew them and a couple could easily have been my real-life neighbors, but I still wasn't invested in them. Overall a good read, but something kept me from really connecting with the story and characters. If you want a book to pass a lazy summer day, this is the one for you."

"I could not put this book down. The Things We Wish Were True follows a group of neighbors over one summer. Everyone is keeping secrets, and they unfold slowly in this character-driven novel. The characters are brought together by a boy’s near-drowning at the neighborhood pool. The book switches between many characters, and that’s what kept my interest since character-driven books aren’t usually my thing."

The characters are quite diverse, but they all have their own struggles. I loved them all. I blew through this book in just a couple of days. I had to know what the secrets were, and I wanted to see everything work out well for all of the characters. This novel was just pure delight to read. I need to get my hands on more books by Marybeth."

"Folks, this little book was a treasure, a journey sometimes pleasant, sometimes perilous, but always gripping, through a bucolic and almost too-good-to-be-true fictitious suburban enclave just outside the very real metropolis of Charlotte, NC. I can attest to the landscape's atmosphere conjured in deft, often lyrical prose by the author--I've been there and seen that back in the day, and it's real enough. Without trying, I believe you can actually smell the chlorine, hot asphalt, cut grass, and hear the sounds of kids at the pool, amplified by the concrete surround and the humid, breathless air."

"I am an avid reader but seldom feel the need or the inspiration to write a review about a novel. Yet, I felt to do some in this instance because this book was unlike any I have ever read. The story is well written and at first hard to follow because of the alternating persons narrating the story. The story begins with the yearly opening of the neighborhood pool. At first, there seems no connection with the characters but as the truth reveals itself piece by piece it is shown that the characters are connected in the story which eachother."
